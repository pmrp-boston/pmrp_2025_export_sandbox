Here is a great opportunity to perform in an exciting new medium:
radio\! Well, okay, radio's not new, but it would be a new experience
for many of you. The radio programs of the Golden Age are an historic
part of our culture. They were an extension of stage and vaudeville that
served as the stepping stone for many actors, singers and production
people who went on to major TV and movie careers. This is a chance to
experience what it was like — and do it in front of an audience\!

Our 2015 Hallowe’en show will be a two-parter, with adaptations of
<cite>Frankenstein</cite> and <cite>Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde</cite>.

There are several types of roles available in the show: from on-stage
acting, to live sound effects to crucial behind-the-scenes positions.
Read on to find out more and then [**click here to sign up for an
audition slot**](http://goo.gl/forms/DF1Oeo4VJt)\!

**PLEASE NOTE:** The Post-Meridian Radio Players is a community theater
group and is run by volunteers. Our performers are not paid nor are we
able to cast Equity actors.

## How to Audition

1.  Check out [**the available roles**](/events/mirror/roles) for each
    segment
2.  Go to [**our audition documents
    folder**](https://drive.google.com/folderview?id=0Bz1RWNejvIxdfmg2Rk9Xc05xZm5ReDdjTTBtTXV0NzRJM0szdHMxcWgyazFOcURJSDlBa2M&usp=sharing)
    on Google Drive. There you will find a paper audition form and
    rehearsal availability grid which you should print out, fill out,
    and bring with you to your audition. You will also find audition
    sides ([definition](http://pmrptest.aq.org/glossary#sides)) which
    you may want to look at ahead of time.
3.  Then, [**click here to sign up for an audition
    appointment**](http://goo.gl/forms/DF1Oeo4VJt), fill out the form,
    and submit it.
4.  If you have submitted the form correctly and one of the time slots
    you requested is still available, you will get email confirming your
    audition appointment. (This is sent by a human being who has a day
    job, so don't worry if it doesn't come right away. But if it doesn’t
    come within a day or so, contact us at auditions@pmrp.org. You can
    also use that address to contact us if you have trouble submitting
    the form.)
5.  Check back to see when the audition sides and available roles are
    posted — you’ll want to read the sides over and perhaps practice
    them before your audition, and you’ll want to put any preferences
    you have for roles on your form when you come. (We’ll try to
    remember to put a blurb on [the front page](/) when those are
    ready.)
6.  Then, show up for your audition at the scheduled time\! Good luck
    and have fun\!

### Some Things to Know

  - The audition process involves one form that you fill out online (the
    appointment form) and two paper forms which you should print out,
    complete, and bring with you to the audition. They’re not
    interchangeable.
  - If you show up for an audition without the paper forms (or the
    sides), we’ll probably have a few extra copies printed out and you
    can fill out the forms there. However, it saves time and trouble and
    makes the process go more smoothly if you can bring completed forms
    with you. (It also helps demonstrate to the directors how
    responsible and resourceful you are\!)
  - **Walk-ins** are welcome, but **people with appointments will take
    priority**. If there are no-shows during a given hour, we will fill
    those slots with walk-ins. Any remaining walk-ins will be auditioned
    at 10pm. It helps the directors tremendously if you sign up in
    advance, though.
