## Backstory

-----

![WPM Radio](/sites/default/files/wpm-mic_01.png)In 1949, the ten-storey
Putnam-Moore Department Store—in what is now Downtown Crossing—closed
its doors and radio station WPM moved its operations to the old
vaudeville theater next door. But it was not the only move made that
year.

On <cite>The Frank Cyrano Byfar Hour</cite>, Frank and Amelia moved into
the Adams family home on Beacon Hill after Amelia's father, Ephraim J.
Adams, left it to her in his will. With this move they also inherited
the Adams' long-suffering butler, Chelmsford, along with an unusual
assortment of neighbors and nuisances.

Domestic life soon proves to be more than a match for Amelia, while
Frank finds that being rich has its advantages...some of the time.
