**Performances:** October 25th, 26th, and 31st and November 1st and 2d
at 8:00pm,  
with an additional matinée on Saturday, October 26th, at 2:00pm.  
**Location:** [Boston Brunch Church (formerly Responsible Grace), 204
Elm Street, Somerville](#location).  
**Tickets and reservations** are available on [our Tickets
page](/tickets).  
**Content note:** In addition to the usual horrors of a Halloween show,
please note that “The Myling” depicts the aftermath of a young child's
death (not portrayed).

![](/sites/default/files/TOTBG_banner-480.jpg)  
This Halloween the Post-Meridian Radio Players are proud to present
<cite>Tomes of Terror: Beyond Grimm</cite>\! Join us for original
adaptations of four creepy tales from around the globe\! We will be
presenting:

**“A Hare-Raising Tale”** by Naomi Hinchen, directed by J. Deschene — A
gambler swears he’ll play cards with anyone, any time. But when he meets
a giant hare on his way home one night, he should be careful not to bet
more than he can lose... *[(Read the dramaturg’s note on this
segment.)](/events/beyondgrimm/dramaturg-notes/#hare)*

**“The Myling”** by Adrian Cory, directed by Tegan Kehoe — In the dead
of winter, a loving family of four is plagued by tales of the Myling –
and the dark secret the parents have been hiding for thirteen years.
*[(Read the dramaturg’s note on this
segment.)](/events/beyondgrimm/dramaturg-notes/#myling)*

**“The Boy Who Drew Cats”** by Greg Lam, directed by Laura Corliss — A
boy is compelled to draw pictures of cats, to the chagrin of everyone
else in his life. But are the cats more than they seem? *[(Read the
dramaturg’s note on this
segment.)](/events/beyondgrimm/dramaturg-notes/#cats)*

**“La Siguanaba”** by Elizabeth Salazar, directed by Joye Thaller — When
a fruit plantation worker’s husband is mysteriously killed, she can’t
shake her curiosity about the creature rumored to have done the deed.
*[(Read the director’s interview of the
playwright.)](/events/beyondgrimm/siguanaba-interview/)*

Performances will run from October 25th through November 2nd at Boston
Brunch Church (formerly Responsible Grace Church).

[The program for this
show](http://pmrp.org/static/pdf/2019-10-TOTBG_Program.pdf) (11MB PDF)
is available for download, and has directors’ notes, music credits, and
additional information (as well as advertisements from our sponsors and
information about upcoming shows).
