**Auditions:** Monday, May 7th, and Tuesday, May 8th, 2018  
at [Boston Brunch Church](#location) in Davis Square, Somerville.  
(three hourlong slots each night at 7:00, 8:00, and 9:00pm)  
[Callbacks](/glossary#callbacks), if needed, will be Thursday, May
10th.  
**Performances:** Performances will run from July 20-28 at Boston Brunch
Church.

-----

Join us this Summer as we present three exciting mysteries: Vera Wolfe
in “The Boy who Cried Wolfe,” a gender-swapped adaptation of a 1950 Nero
Wolfe radio broadcast, Arsène Lupin in “Sherlock Holmes Arrives Too
Late,” and Sherlock Holmes and Dr. Watson in ”The Final Problem.” (See
[the event’s own page](/events/srt18) for a little more about the
segments.) Performances will run from July 20-28 at Boston Brunch Church
(formerly Responsible Grace Church).

There are several types of positions available in the show: from
on-stage acting, to live sound effects to crucial behind-the-scenes
positions. Read on to find out more and then [click here to sign up for
an audition slot](https://goo.gl/forms/bUpmGh4wXXQLiTKJ2).

**PLEASE NOTE:** The Post-Meridian Radio Players is a community theater
group and is run by volunteers. Our performers are not paid.

-----

## How to Audition

1.  Check out [**the available
    roles**](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1hCXGv4oCQ_0EIKh8IfPhA5ilqBRdur0M)
    and think a little bit about what you’re interested in. (That
    includes voice roles, of course, but also live sound-effects
    performance, tech crew, and various other things.) Some recurring
    roles, including Holmes, Watson, and Lupin, are pre-cast, but most
    are available.
2.  [**Click here to sign up for an audition
    appointment**](https://goo.gl/forms/bUpmGh4wXXQLiTKJ2), fill out the
    form, and submit it.
3.  If you have submitted the form correctly and one of the time slots
    you requested is still available, you will get email confirming your
    audition appointment. (This is sent by a human being who has a day
    job, so don't worry if it doesn't come right away. But if it doesn’t
    come within a day or so, contact us at auditions@pmrp.org. You can
    also use that address to contact us if you have trouble submitting
    the form.)
4.  Then come back in a few days and check [**our audition documents
    folder**](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/13J7AR4xoasRHire3c0G86lbT_X0zY4Vn?usp=sharing)
    on Google Drive. There you will find a paper audition form and
    rehearsal availability grid which you should print out, fill out,
    and bring with you to your audition. (If you forget, we’ll have
    spares for you to fill out at the audition.) Those aren’t ready yet,
    but they'll be posted well before auditions.
5.  [You’ll eventually also
    find](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/13J7AR4xoasRHire3c0G86lbT_X0zY4Vn?usp=sharing)
    audition sides for the three segments, including the available
    roles. You’ll want to look over the list of roles and decide which
    ones you’re most interested in, and look over (and perhaps practice)
    the sides before your audition. (Only the “Final Problem” sides are
    up yet, but we’ll be adding sides for the other two segments
    shortly.)
6.  Then, show up for your audition at the scheduled time\! Good luck
    and have fun\!

### Some Things to Know

  - The audition process involves one form that you fill out online (the
    appointment form) and two paper forms (not available just yet) which
    you should print out, complete, and bring with you to the audition.
    They’re not interchangeable.
  - If you show up for an audition without the paper forms (or the
    sides), we’ll probably have a few extra copies printed out and you
    can fill out the forms there. However, it saves time and trouble and
    makes the process go more smoothly if you can bring completed forms
    with you. (It also helps demonstrate to the directors how
    responsible and resourceful you are\!)
  - **Walk-ins** are welcome, but **people with appointments will take
    priority**. If there are no-shows during a given hour, we will fill
    those slots with walk-ins. Any remaining walk-ins will be auditioned
    at 10pm. It helps the directors tremendously if you sign up in
    advance, though.

## Foley Artists (Live Sound Effects)

If you’re interested in being a Foley performer and making the live
sound effects, there will be a Foley interview at the time of your
audition slot. You can absolutely both audition for a voice role and
express interest in being a Foley artist (although you can only *do* one
or the other, since voice actors and Foley artists are performing
simultaneously). Just let us know on your form what roles and/or
positions you want to be considered for.
