**Auditions:** Thursday, February 7th, 2019.  
**Recording:** Friday, March 8th, 2019.  
**Audition location:** [Boston Brunch Church (formerly Responsible
Grace), 204 Elm Street, Somerville](#location)

-----

<cite>PMRP Presents</cite> is proud to present an original adaptation of
Charlotte Perkins Gilman's short story "The Yellow Wallpaper." A young
wife is brought to a secluded estate for three months of complete and
total rest. But as the stifling stillness goes on she discovers there
may be far more going on behind the images on the wall.

Auditions will be on Thursday February 7th at Boston Brunch Church. Want
to audition? Read on for more information, and then fill out the form
below to request an audition slot.

“The Yellow Wallpaper” was adapted for PMRP by Director Amy
Bennett-Zendzian.

Produced by Chris DeKalb

The show will be recorded on Friday March 8th for general release.

Please note this is an unpaid project.  

## Characters

**“Charlotte”** (never named in dialogue) — a young wife and mother,
20s  
**John** — her husband, a doctor, 40s  
**Jennie** — their housekeeper, John’s sister, 30s

All three are 19th-century upper-class Americans. The story takes place
over three months, somewhere in New England in 1885.  

## How to Audition

1.  Check out the available roles above and think a little bit about
    what you’re interested in — there also positions for sound-effects
    performance, tech crew, and various other things.
2.  Then go to [**our audition documents
    folder**](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1A6scxavE7JIaXj_UhSZNZmPM7pAv2j_O?usp=sharing)
    on Google Drive. There you will find a paper audition form and
    rehearsal availability grid which you should print out, fill out,
    and bring with you to your audition.
3.  Then, [**click here to sign up for an audition
    appointment**](https://goo.gl/forms/bPTKn5iOp4AQjbfy2), fill out the
    form, and submit it.
4.  If you have submitted the form correctly and one of the time slots
    you requested is still available, you will get email confirming your
    audition appointment. (This is sent by a human being who has a day
    job, so don't worry if it doesn't come right away. But if it doesn’t
    come within a day or so, contact us at auditions@pmrp.org. You can
    also use that address to contact us if you have trouble submitting
    the form.)
5.  Then print out (or download to your tablet) the audition sides from
    [our Google Drive Audition Docs
    folder](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1A6scxavE7JIaXj_UhSZNZmPM7pAv2j_O?usp=sharing).
    You’ll want to look over and/or rehearse them before your audition,
    and it will be very helpful if you can bring your copy to the
    audition (although we’ll have a few extra copies printed out).
6.  Then, show up for your audition at the scheduled time\! Please bring
    your filled-out paper audition form and availability schedule. Good
    luck and have fun\!

### Some Things to Know

  - The audition process involves one form that you fill out online (the
    appointment form) and two paper forms which you should print out,
    complete, and bring with you to the audition. They’re not
    interchangeable. (The paper forms are not yet posted.)
  - If you show up for an audition without the paper forms (or the
    sides), we’ll probably have a few extra copies printed out and you
    can fill out the forms there. However, it saves time and trouble and
    makes the process go more smoothly if you can bring completed forms
    with you. (It also helps demonstrate to the directors how
    responsible and resourceful you are\!)
  - **Walk-ins** are welcome, but **people with appointments will take
    priority**. If there are no-shows during a given hour, we will fill
    those slots with walk-ins. Any remaining walk-ins will be auditioned
    at 10pm. It helps the directors tremendously if you sign up in
    advance, though.
