## PMRP Summer Radio Theatre

### July 14th–16th, 2011

-----

![Summer Radio Theatre](/sites/default/files/srt11-header.jpg)  

For the first time ever, **The Post-Meridian Radio Players** present a
live summer show\! In the tradition of our original <cite>Tomes of
Terror</cite> Halloween thrillogies, we present three half-hour radio
dramas of suspense, adventure and thrills to add excitement to your
summer\!

-----

## <span id="featuring"></span>Featuring

-----

<span class="banner">![Black Jack Justice: The Do-Nothing
Detective](/sites/default/files/srt11-bjj.png)</span>  

> *When a mystery man pays private detectives Jack Justice and Trixie
> Dixon a hefty retainer to drop a client they never had, it looks like
> they finally caught that nice soft case every detective dreams of. But
> it couldn't really be that simple...could it?*

Written by Gregg Taylor  
Directed by Mindy Klenoff

A product of the Toronto-based audio drama producer [Decoder Ring
Theatre](http://www.decoderringtheatre.com/), this series features tales
from the casebook of that master of mystery, that sultan of sleuthing,
Martin Bracknell's immortal detective [Black Jack
Justice](http://www.decoderringtheatre.com/index.php?id=bjj). **The
Post-Meridian Radio Players** are grateful to the folks at DCT, and to
BJJ creator Gregg Taylor for permission to perform one of the episodes
from this fantastic series.

<span class="banner">![X Minus One:
Junkyard](/sites/default/files/srt11-xm1.png)</span>  

> *An episode of the classic 1950's sci-fi series, based on a short
> story written by Clifford D. Simak, in which the crew of a rocketship
> land on an obscure planetoid whose salient features are a junkyard of
> scrap parts from another ship and a mysterious tower.*

Based on the short story by Clifford D. Simak  
Adapted for radio by George Lefferts  
Directed by Michael McAfee

<cite>[X Minus One](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/X_Minus_One)</cite> and
its predecessor, <cite>[Dimension
X](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimension_X)</cite>, are fine examples
of why radio could be considered the perfect medium for science fiction.
From 1955 to 1958, this NBC series featured dramatizatons of stories
from authors we consider today to be the Masters of SF: Asimov,
Bradbury, Dick, Heinlein, Pohl and Sturgeon, among many others.

-----

<span class="banner">![Escape\! Three Skeleton
Key](/sites/default/files/srt11-esc.png)</span>  

> *One of the all-time classic stories of old-time radio, originally
> featuring Vincent Price. After a derelict ship runs aground on their
> reef, three lighthouse keepers must fight for their lives against
> thousands of starving rats\!*

Based on the short story by George Toudouze  
Adapted for radio by James Poe  
Directed by Heidi Clark

From 1947 to 1954,
<cite>[Escape](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Escape_\(radio_program\))</cite>
was the leading high-adventure anthology series on radio and featured
the popular voice actor William Conrad (later to play Marshal Matt
Dillon on the radio version of <cite>Gunsmoke</cite> and Frank Cannon in
the 1970s TV detective series <cite>Cannon</cite>). After Orson Welles'
<cite>War of the Worlds</cite> broadcast, "Three Skeleton Key" is,
perhaps, the most famous radio drama story of all time. Between
<cite>Escape</cite> and its sister show,
<cite>[Suspense](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suspense_%28radio_program%29)</cite>
(1942–1962), audience demand compelled producers to air the story no
less than five times\!
