This Halloween the Post-Meridian Radio Players are proud to present
[<cite>The Van Helsing Chronicles</cite>](/events/vanhelsing)\! Famed
monster hunter Professor A. Van Helsing hosts and narrates two tales of
Gothic horror in which he eventually also plays a role. We shall be
presenting two stories, original adaptations of Richard Marsh's **The
Beetle** and Bram Stoker's **Dracula**. There will be several types of
positions available in the show, including on-stage voice acting, live
Foley sound-effects performance, and crucial behind-the-scenes
positions.

Auditions will be on **Monday, August 13, and Tuesday, August 14**, with
callbacks (if needed) on Thursday, August 16. **Scroll down to learn how
to sign up for a slot and audition.**

Parts will be available for both voice and Foley roles.

Performances will be two weekends from **October 19-27**.

## How to Audition

1.  Check out [**the available
    roles**](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1WnvjVZkpTLsJFSxWFC_Sc8hJAaf2Ror-_fa7jHMg6KQ/edit?usp=sharing)
    (Google Docs link) and think a little bit about what you’re
    interested in — the voice roles listed on that page are available,
    but there also positions for sound-effects performance, tech crew,
    and various other things.
2.  Then go to [**our audition documents
    folder**](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1NTqkOs6rBozmsMMdGOXy47ZVC3IfH0VM)
    on Google Drive. There you will find a paper audition form and
    rehearsal availability grid which you should print out, fill out,
    and bring with you to your audition. You’ll also find a description
    of the characters in the various segments, so you can decide which
    roles you’re most interested in. As they’re ready, we’ll also put
    audition sides ([definition](http://pmrptest.aq.org/glossary#sides))
    there, which you’ll probably want to look at ahead of time.
3.  Then, [**click here to sign up for an audition
    appointment**](https://goo.gl/forms/DpjkB3zizd9jHxnq2), fill out the
    form, and submit it.
4.  If you have submitted the form correctly and one of the time slots
    you requested is still available, you will get email confirming your
    audition appointment. (This is sent by a human being who has a day
    job, so don't worry if it doesn't come right away. But if it doesn’t
    come within a day or so, contact us at auditions@pmrp.org. You can
    also use that address to contact us if you have trouble submitting
    the form.)
5.  Come back here in a week or so to check for additional documents
    we’ll want you to print and fill out and bring with you to your
    audition slot, indicating what roles you’re interested in and what
    your availability is for rehearsals. (We’ll also have a list of
    available roles with descriptions by then.) Don’t wait for these to
    be posted, though\! Go ahead and sign up for your audition slot now,
    since slots can fill up.
6.  Before your audition, go to [**our audition documents
    folder**](https://drive.google.com/open?id=1NTqkOs6rBozmsMMdGOXy47ZVC3IfH0VM)
    to download audition sides
    ([definition](http://pmrptest.aq.org/glossary#sides)), which you’ll
    probably want to look at ahead of time (not yet available). We also
    have a list of available roles.
7.  Then, show up for your audition at the scheduled time\! Good luck
    and have fun\!

### Some Things to Know

  - The audition process involves one form that you fill out online (the
    appointment form) and two paper forms which you should print out,
    complete, and bring with you to the audition. They’re not
    interchangeable. (The paper forms are not up yet.)
  - If you show up for an audition without the paper forms (or the
    sides), we’ll probably have a few extra copies printed out and you
    can fill out the forms there. However, it saves time and trouble and
    makes the process go more smoothly if you can bring completed forms
    with you. (It also helps demonstrate to the directors how
    responsible and resourceful you are\!)
  - **Walk-ins** are welcome, but **people with appointments will take
    priority**. If there are no-shows during a given hour, we will fill
    those slots with walk-ins. Any remaining walk-ins will be auditioned
    at 10pm. It helps the directors tremendously if you sign up in
    advance, though.
