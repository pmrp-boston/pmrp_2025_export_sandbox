Our spring 2025 show, [Dangerous Adventures](/events/dangerous),
presents three tales of adventure\! See [its show
page](/events/dangerous) for more about the three audio dramas.

Read on for information about auditions and the form to sign up for an
audition slot\!

Auditions will be **Monday, February 3, and Tuesday, February 4, 2025**
at [Guild Church](venues/204elm) in Somerville. **Scroll down to learn

## How to Audition

### Now

1.  Then, [**click here to sign up for an audition
    appointment**](https://forms.gle/13XVJerAKtTsbcgF9), fill out the
    form, and submit it.
2.  If you have submitted the form correctly and one of the time slots
    you requested is still available, you will get email confirming your
    audition appointment. (This is sent by a human being who has a day
    job, so don't worry if it doesn't come right away. But if it doesn’t
    come within a day or so, contact us at auditions@pmrp.org. You can
    also use that address to contact us if you have trouble submitting
    the form.)
3.  Then check [our Audition Documents folder on Google
    Drive](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/17bGmyKFSM0gQF6WJ5WdPAFqt-6S4waAY?usp=sharing)
    and read over the synopses and character descriptions for all three
    plays and think a little bit about what you’re interested in — the
    voice roles listed there are available, but there also positions for
    sound-effects performance, tech crew, and various other things.
    **(The synopses and character descriptions have all now been
    posted\!)** Note that “The Most Dangerous Game” is gender-swapped
    from the original story, with all characters portrayed as women.

### Before your audition

1.  Print out (or download to your tablet) the audition sides from [our
    Google Drive Audition Docs
    folder](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/17bGmyKFSM0gQF6WJ5WdPAFqt-6S4waAY?usp=sharing).
    You’ll want to look over and/or rehearse them before your audition,
    and it will be very helpful if you can bring your copy to the
    audition (although we’ll have a few extra copies printed out). Sides
    and descriptions for all three segments have now been posted.
2.  Then fill out **[this auditionee information
    form](https://forms.gle/wsLRAdJSD13hkkUXA)**, which lists things
    like which roles or radio-plays you're interested in, schedule
    availability, whether you're interested in Foley performance roles
    or behind-the-scenes positions, and so on.
3.  Then, show up for your audition at the scheduled time\! Please bring
    your filled-out paper audition form and availability schedule. Good
    luck and have fun\!

### Some Things to Know

  - Voice rehearsals will generally be on Tuesdays. For the first few
    weeks, Foley and voice actors will be rehearsing separately, with
    Foley rehearsals on Mondays. After the first three weeks or so,
    voice and Foley will rejoin and rehearse together on Tuesdays.
  - The audition process involves two different online forms [one to
    schedule you for an audition
    slot](https://forms.gle/13XVJerAKtTsbcgF9) (the appointment form)
    and [another one (the auditionee information
    form)](https://forms.gle/wsLRAdJSD13hkkUXA) that directors and stage
    managers will use when casting, contacting you about the results of
    your audition, scheduling rehearsals, and so on. They’re not
    interchangeable.
  - If you show up for an audition without the auditionee information
    form filled out (or the sides), we’ll probably have a paper copy you
    can fill out and we can transcribe, or a QR code for the form so you
    can fill it out on your phone. However, it saves time and trouble
    and makes the process go more smoothly if you submit the form before
    your audition. (It also helps demonstrate to the directors how
    responsible and resourceful you are\!)
  - Similarly, if you don't have sides printed out (or on a tablet)
    we'll have some copies auditionees can borrow, but it's best if you
    can print them out or have them on a device you can comfortably read
    from.
  - **Walk-ins** are welcome, but **people with appointments will take
    priority**. If there are no-shows during a given hour, we will fill
    those slots with walk-ins. Any remaining walk-ins will be auditioned
    at 10pm. It helps the directors tremendously if you sign up in
    advance, though.
