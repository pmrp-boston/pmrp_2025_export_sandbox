THE POST-MERIDIAN RADIO PLAYERS are bringing one of America's most
respected authors to our stage this Halloween as we present <cite>Tomes
of Terror: Nevermore</cite>.

Three tales of Edgar Allen Poe will grace our stage—and your ears—this
Fall, including adaptations of his classic stories <cite>The Cask of
Amontillado</cite> and <cite>The Fall of the House of Usher</cite>.

But for the third...well, that's where you come in\!

-----

## Call for Scripts

When the shadows grow long and the winds blow cold, THE POST-MERIDIAN
RADIO PLAYERS pry open the creaking gates of The Library to unleash
tales of horror and woe upon the peoples of Boston. These chilling
stories and dark morality plays form a trilogy known as <cite>Tomes of
Terror</cite>.

PMRP is now accepting submissions for the third story in our Poe
trilogy, Tomes of Terror: Nevermore. We're looking for an original
adaptation of an Edgar Allen Poe story. Aside from the two we already
have, any Poe story is acceptable.

Poe stories with some revisions, such as gender-swapping the characters,
changing the time period or location, will be accepted (in fact we
encourage female characters in your story\!).

Pastiches and “Poe-like” stories may be submitted but direct story
adaptations are strongly preferred. While some humor is fine, we are not
looking for parodies.

We are not looking for documentary, biography or non-fiction pieces.  

-----

## Further Details

  - Submissions should be completed scripts in radio drama format. This
    performance uses live Foley performers as well, so remember the
    sound effects.
  - Your play should run 22-25 minutes.
  - Due to stage size limitations, there will be a maximum of 5 voice
    actors on mic at any one time.
  - Crowd scenes do not count towards this limit.
  - We understand that this is both a horror show and Edgar Allen Poe,
    but gore and adult content should be kept to a reasonable level. So
    PG-13 is fine, R is okay but NC-17 is right out.
  - Previously produced material is allowed, providing the author
    retains the rights to the script.
  - Multiple submissions from the same author(s) are allowed.
  - Scripts may be revised as needed for the performance and PMRP
    retains all performance rights including director choice, casting
    and sound design.
