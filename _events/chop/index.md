![Tomes of Terror II](/sites/default/files/chop-lglogo.png)  
<span class="lightblue">THE POST-MERIDIAN RADIO PLAYERS</span> will be
re-creating Arch Oboler's lost <cite>Lights Out\!</cite> episode
“Chicken Heart” as part of <cite>Chicken Hearts & Other Parts</cite>:
a Hallowe’en-themed fundraiser for local filmmaker Chelsea Spear.  The
cast includes a team of 15 actors providing voices for 32 characters,
and two live effects artists who will utilize everything from a
stainless steel mixing bowl and a lunch box to a hand-held sewing
machine and a vat of gelatin to convince the audience that a chicken's
heart is, indeed, devouring the world around them.

-----

## Featuring

**The Atlantis Affair:**  A live sci-fi/horror tragicomic radio drama,
by local writer/director Alex Feinman, about the surreal encounters of a
Boston-based computer programmer and his companions with the mysterious.
Performed by members of [Silicon
Theatre](http://www.silicontheatre.com/).  
**Erasure:**  A reading by local author [Jennifer
Pelland](http://www.jenniferpelland.com) of her original short story
about a woman struggling to understand who she is after her memories of
a traumatic experience have been erased—and the dangers she now faces.  
**Chicken Heart:**  The <span class="graybold">PMRP</span> re-create
this lost episode from the classic radio series, <cite>Lights
Out\!</cite>, written by Arch Oboler in 1937 and immortalized decades
later by Bill Cosby in one of his most famous comedy routines.  
**Improv PoetryProse:**  Horrific dreamtellings woven by
nationally-recognized voice artist Lindsay Ellison, based on the books
at hand and topics and images suggested by the audience.  
**The Unhappy Medium:**  Screening of a segment from local filmmaker
Chelsea Spear's current project, [The Ballad of Burd
Janet](http://www.pixievision.com). A re-telling of the Celtic folktale
[Tam Lin](http://www.tam-lin.org/), set in New England of the 1920s.
