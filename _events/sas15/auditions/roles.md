## The Mysterious Traveler

Adapted and Directed by Christopher Chiampa  
No accents required, other than a fake elderly voice by Lupin.

  - **The Gentleman** (m, 20s-40s):  
    A famous French gentleman-thief. A classic rogue and master of
    disguise who answers to no one but his own whims. Actor must be able
    to switch between an "old" and "young" voice.
  - **The Lady** (f. mid-30s-50s preferred):  
      
    An adventuress; brilliant and cool-headed, she knows more about
    everyone around her than she lets on.
  - **The Thief** (m, any age):  
    A looming henchman who has been sent on a secret errand; his target
    and purpose unknown to all but himself. Total opposite of the
    others; crass, quick to anger, and a bit dull-witted.

## The Scarlet Pimpernel

Adapted and Directed by Elizabeth Salazar  
**Note about Accents:** English accents will be used for English roles;
French accents will be preferred for French roles (coaching will be
available).  

### Major Roles

  - **Marguerite St. Just** (younger female):  
    French former actress and revolutionary, now a charming trendsetter
    in the English court. Unsatisfied with her marriage to a fool,
    haunted by a past mistake.
  - **Sir Percy Blakeney, the Scarlet Pimpernel** (late 20s/30s,
    male):  
    English baronet who plays the foolish fop at court. Rescuer of
    French aristocrats, master of disguise. Requires multiple voices.
  - **Citizen Chauvelin** (30s+, any gender):  
    Cold and keen-eyed agent of France who hunts the Scarlet Pimpernel.
  - **Suzanne de Tournay** (20s, female):  
    Aristocratic French refugee and old friend of Marguerite’s.
    Faithful, though with a small rebellious streak.
  - **Sir Andrew Ffoulkes** (younger male):  
    English noble in the League of the Scarlet Pimpernel and Percy’s
    right-hand man. An earnest gentleman.

### Ensemble Roles

  - **Comtesse de Tournay** (older female):  
    A poised French aristocrat in exile. Protective of her daughter
    Suzanne.
  - **Brogard** (older, any gender):  
    Surly French innkeeper who grumbles about pretty much everyone.
  - **Desgas** (any age, any gender):  
    Chauvelin’s lackey. Obedient but afraid of Chauvelin’s displeasure.
  - **Armand St. Just** (younger male):  
    Marguerite’s brother. A member of the League of the Scarlet
    Pimpernel and Percy’s main ally in France.
  - **Lord Grenville** (older male):  
    British Foreign Secretary of State. A reluctant host to Chauvelin.
  - **Lady Portarles** (any age, female):  
    English aristocrat, kind-hearted but honest.
  - **Prince of Wales** (30s, male):  
    The future King George IV. Jovial, friend of the Blakeneys.
  - **Comte de Tournay** (older male):  
    French noble hoping to escape the guillotine.

### Additional Voices

**Nobles**, **servants**, **soldiers**, and **crowds**.
